### **Card 2: ZOPA Framework - Know Your Boundaries**
**Type:** Educational Framework (no XP, pure learning)  
**XP:** 0 points  
**Duration:** 8 min

**Content:**
Visual diagram of ZOPA (Zone of Possible Agreement):

```
YOUR SIDE:                                    THEIR SIDE:
Walk-away price: 80k PLN â†---ZOPA---â†’ Max budget: 120k PLN
Target price: 100k PLN                Best case: 85k PLN
BATNA: Sell to competitor             BATNA: Buy from Bosch at 70k
```

**4 Key Concepts:**
1. **BATNA (Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement)**
   - Your BATNA: What you do if deal fails (e.g., sell to another client, walk away)
   - Their BATNA: What they do if deal fails (e.g., buy from Bosch, delay purchase)
   - **Power Rule:** Whoever has better BATNA has more negotiation power

2. **Reservation Price**
   - Your walk-away price: Below this, deal is unprofitable
   - Their max budget: Above this, they can't afford
   - ZOPA exists when Your min < Their max

3. **Target Price vs. Anchor**
   - Target: Your ideal outcome (e.g., 100k PLN)
   - Anchor: First number mentioned (sets the range)
   - **Golden Rule:** Always anchor high (but justifiable with ROI)

4. **Value Creation vs. Value Claiming**
   - Value Creation: Expand the pie (add services, training, longer warranty)
   - Value Claiming: Split the pie (discount, payment terms)
   - **Top KAM:** Create first, claim second

**Interactive Element:**
Comparison table: Weak Negotiator vs. Strong Negotiator

| Scenario | Weak Negotiator | Strong Negotiator |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Client says "Too expensive" | Gives 15% discount immediately | Asks: "Compared to what? Let's review ROI calculation" |
| CFO asks for 20% off | Offers 10% compromise | Offers: 5% discount + 6-month leasing + free training (same margin, higher perceived value) |
| Procurement threatens Bosch | Lowers price to match | Says: "Bosch at 70k = 43k TCO/3yr. Milwaukee 100k = 23k TCO. You'd lose 20k to save 30k upfront. Shall we pilot both?" |
| Deadline pressure | Panics, gives concessions | Uses deadline: "I can lock this price until Friday, then Q2 pricing +8% kicks in" |
